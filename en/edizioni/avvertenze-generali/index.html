General Instructions

  • 1. Editorial Guidelines
    1. The guidelines include as integral part the instructions for the composition of text files, abstract and bibliography and are available for download at http://www.edizionicafoscari.unive.it/linee_guida.php.
    2. The observance of the editorial guidelines is essential for texts’ acceptance. The Publisher reserves the right to interrupt the publication – at any time – in case of lacking observance of the guidelines.
    3. Each paper must include an abstract, not less than 3 keywords (both in English) and a selected and specific bibliography (except for a monographic volume belonging to one single author: in this case, there will be a single abstract and bibliography for the whole volume). Should ECF consider the abstract’s language quality unsatisfactory, the Author would commit – at his own expense – to a revision made by a qualified mother tongue translator.
  • 2. Editorial Workflow
    1. he paper’s submission to ECF can be carried out exclusively by the use of the peer-review platform purposely made available by the Publishing House. Edizioni Ca' Foscari provides with the necessary instructions regarding the platform’ usage, also by means of specific meetings with the subjects involved. Each journal and book series have their own specific profile within the platform and the directors take charge of creating Authors’ and Reviewers’ profiles (with particular attention to their affiliation), keeping them up-to-date.
    2. The Editorial Staff verifies the observance of the guidelines and reserves the right to reject and request an adaptation of the faulty texts.
    3. Except for different agreements with the Scientific Committee of a journal/book series, it is necessary to submit each contribution to a double and anonymous peer-review process (see the Certification of Scientific Works)
    4. To ensure the integrity of the blind peer-review for submission to the conference, every effort should be made to prevent the identities of the Authors and Reviewers from being known to each other.
      Directors, and Reviewers (who upload documents as part of their review) checking to see if the following steps have been taken with regard to the text and the file properties:
      • all the information that could lead to a personal identification. Authors will refer to themselves exclusively by using the word “Author” ;
      • all the information included in the file’s “Properties”. If working with text editing programmes, such as Microsoft Word, OpenOffice, LibreOffice, please follow these steps: Main Menu > File > Properties > Remove personal information > Save .
    5. Each paper must be assigned to the reviewers exclusively by the Scientific Committee of the journal/book series through ECF platform. The observance of the reviews’ deadlines is under the responsibility of the scientific committee.
    6. Should the reviews’ outcome request one or more modifications of the text, the Author would commit to upload a second draft of his paper within 10 days.
    7. ECF submits to the Author only one round of drafts, in PDF format, which the Author commits to send back within 8 days. Corrections must be marked within the PDF file by using Adobe Acrobat’s specific tools only.
    8. Between the conclusion of the reviews and the publication (as long as the arranged deadlines are respected) at least 8 weeks are forecast to pass.
  • 3. Contents
    • Should non-Latin languages be used, the Author commits to verify their compatibility with ECF fonts (http://www.edizionicafoscari.unive.it/linee_guida.php) and to contact the Publishing house promptly and in advance.
    • In case of graphic elements such as:
      • Figures: high-resolution files are requested for the print (min. 300 dpi on 14 cm). Each figure must be followed by a capture including subject, author, date and source.
      • The publication of the above-mentioned figures can be carried out only upon a consent form signed by the Author. The specific form is here available for download and must be sent to ECF before the editing process starts.
      • Graphics: they must be adapted to ECF fonts or created with the procedure suggested by the editorial staff, which can vary case by case.
      • The Publisher reserves the right to reject any content, which is not in compliance with the guidelines.
  • 4. Print on demand
    1. The request must reach the Publishing House before the review-process starts, including the print-run indication desired.
    2. The estimate will be given when the first drafts are sent back to the editorial staff.
    3. The delivery of the printed copies requested occurs with a bimonthly periodicity, according to the calendar: February, April, June, September and December.
    4. The copies requested are exclusively delivered to the department in charge, which provides to send at least 10 copies to ECF by means of inner delivery service.
  • 5. Fruition of Published Papers
    1. The papers submitted to Edizioni Ca' Foscari are intended for exclusive publication on this website or on paper, on demand, under the sign “Edizioni Ca' Foscari”.
    2. Any other fruition or purpose of the publication lies outside Edizioni Ca' Foscari’s jurisdiction and is under the Editors’/Authors’ responsibility.
